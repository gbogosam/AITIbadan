Census: NPC to engage 7,000 ad-hoc staff in Kwara.

Ahead of the 2023 Census Population headcount in Nigeria, the National Population Commission (NPC) in Kwara State says about seven thousand ad-hoc staff have been hired for the exercise across the sixteen local government areas of the state. The Federal Commissioner in charge of NPC in Kwara State, Abdulrazaq Gidado disclosed this during an exclusive interview with AIT in Ilorin the State capital where he noted that the exercise would not only cover headcount of human but structures and social amenities inclusive. Population census in any developing or developed country helps the government to determine her statistics level of unemployment in the country, standard of living of the people through per capita income, determine reliable data to formulate policies of the economy amongst others. According to the Federal Commissioner of National Population Commission (NPC) in Kwara State, Abdulrazaq Gidado, the 2023 census population is coming with a difference as not only humans would be counted, buildings and social amenities in all the nooks and crannies of the sixteen local government areas would also be counted with a view to helping government make accurate and timely decisions with seven thousand ad-hoc staff already hired to carry out the exercise.
