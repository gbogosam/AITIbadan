INEC To Resume Collation Of Results At Central Plateau Senetorial District After Declaring Election Inconclusive.

The Independent National Electoral Commission (INEC) has disclosed that will resume collation to conclude the Process for the Plateau Central Senetorial District Election which was declared inconclusive following issues that Marred the Collation of Results.

Plateau State INEC Resident Electoral Commissioner Oliver Gundu disclosed this during a Meeting with the leadership of All Political Parties and other critical stakeholders in the State.

The Results for the controversial Plateau Central Senetorial District Election was not declared, as a result, no return was made due to issues which needed further interrogation. This perhaps is the reason for this meeting with critical stakeholders involved for a Sensitization on the need to conclude the Process as supplementary elections are expected to hold this Saturday 15, April Across the Federation.

The Plateau INEC Resident Electoral Commissioner Oliver Gundu, also disclosed that the Commission is willing to appoint a new returning officer should the Person who was in charge during collation declines.

Stakeholders while commending INEC, also Pledged their cooperation towards the conclusion of the controversial Plateau South Senetorial election.

Recall, that the Plateau Central Senetorial District Election was declared inconclusive due to issues that Marred the collation of results
